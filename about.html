As a child, I lived with my parents in French Algeria. French citizen, recently arrived from Paris, I worked as a professor at College de Notre Dame in Versailles, at Lycee Charlemagne in Paris, at the Distance Education Department of Sorbonne Nouvelle University and volunteered my time at Brookhaven National Laboratory in NY state (see p. 3 of this BNL publication) as well as at the SchoolPlus at Stony Brook.
 
I obtained my Master 2 diploma in distance learning engineering in the domain of languages, literature arts, art and contemporary society from Sorbonne Nouvelle University, Paris, France. 
At the same time, I was working at the Department of Distant Education of the University, helping faculty to develop online presence and customizing a Liferay CMS to create eSorbonne, a multimedia broadcasting site of the University. Professors at the Doctorate School at Sorbonne University were interested in my Masters thesis, so they proposed that I enroll in the Ph.D. program at Sorbonne. The subject of my dissertation was Using art for knowledge transmission on educational Internet sites.
 
Now residing on Long Island in the U.S., I am in the Ph.D. program on educational technologies at Stony Brook University, and also working as Instructional Technologist/Professor at Suffolk County Community College. I am overseeing the development of the Virtual Learning Commons, a separate online area where students can freely use learning objects and a social networking space.
 
In my spare time I write and publish fiction. One of my books, Matouchka de Tatiana Yva, is on sell in the Editeurs Reunis Library, 11 rue de la Montagne St Geneviève, 75006 PARIS.
In 2006 I was awarded the Sorbonne literary contest prize for a novel in French, Toi.
 
Favorite passtime is editing a free online learning site of French as a foreign language.
 
Academic Interests
 
I hold a Master’s degree in education from the Sorbonne University, Paris, France, in languages, literature arts, art and contemporary society. Previously graduated with honors in languages & translation from Moscow Linguistic University. I have passed New York State Teachers Certification Examinations.
 
At present, I am working on PhD dissertation at Stony Brook University, Department of Technology & Society, which allows me to reconsiliate my Bachelor in Computer Sciences and Master in Education degrees, as well as my expertise of software developer with the one of college professor. In my latest online session, at Campus Technology Forum 2012, I have presented, together with my colleagues, a positive impact of the Virtual Learning Commons' language and sciences materials on student success.
 
Career Interests
 
The United States hold an incontestable leadership position in the domain of educational technologies, and that is why I choose to continue my research work in a US university. Doing so, I could bring the new approaches and technologies to the distance learning institutions in France, were I would like to develop distance learning partnerships with American universities.
 
I am looking for a position of Faculty and/or Instructional Designer in online education area.
 
Besides, after being for many years web programmer and then professor, I would like to work in the field related to various aspects of information processing on the Internet courseware.
 
Any suggestions of research collaboration in this field are very welcome :)
